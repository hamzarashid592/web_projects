My Story
Avanza Solutions
Software Engineer, Since November 2020

Avanza Solutions is one of Pakistan’s biggest FinTech solution provider. The major clientele includes some of the largest banks of Pakistan as well as abroad. My responsibilities include working with their banking switch i.e. Rendezvous and Nimbus which is their ATM controller.
My major assignments up till now have been with Meezan Bank Limited, MBL which is the largest Islamic bank of Pakistan.
During my tenure here I have done the following RFC's:

  • MBL Direct Biller:

    The aim of this RFC was to enable direct communication between the bank and billing companies and remove the involvement of the billing aggregator. By removing this involvement, both the parties are now able to save the per transaction fee which previously had to go to the aggregator.

  • MBL Raast/MPG Phase II:

    The aim of this RFC was to introduce internet banking P2P payments via an easy to remember alias rather than an account number or IBAN. This enabled customers to use the beneficiary’s cell number to carry on with their payments rather than remembering a 14 digit account number or 34 digit IBAN.
    This was actually a small counterpart of a series of mandates put down by the State Bank of Pakistan, SBP with the aim of centralizing and digitizing the country’s banking industry.
    MPG or Raast itself is sort of a country switch deployed at SBP. For more information click here.

  • MBL Wemsol Integration:

    Keenu Wallet is Digital Wallet service developed by Wemsol Private Limited. In this RFC, the requirement was to facilitate Keenu customers to control their digital wallets via ATM machines. This assignment opened up a new and exciting domain for me as it gave me a gist of how ATM’s work, how ATM’s communicate with the controller and how the controller talks to the middleware. Since, Keenu uses biometric for its customer’s authentication, I only had to deal with the biometric flow concerning the ATM.

Urdu OCR System

During my time at the university my Final Year Project was an OCR system for Urdu language (National language of Pakistan) which I implemented in Python.

This project had less accuracy but it was built entirely from scratch and further work is still being done at the university's research lab to enhance it.
